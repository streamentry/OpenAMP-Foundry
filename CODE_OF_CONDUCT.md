# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

OpenAMP Foundry is a safety-constrained scientific infrastructure project.

The community standard is not only interpersonal respect. It is also scientific honesty, safety awareness, and careful claim discipline.

## Expected behavior

Contributors are expected to:

- be respectful and constructive;
- prefer evidence over confidence;
- preserve caveats and limitations;
- welcome negative results;
- avoid overstating computational outputs;
- respect safety and release boundaries;
- respect data licenses and attribution;
- make work reproducible where practical;
- help new humans and agents contribute safely.

## Unacceptable behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- harassment, abuse, or exclusionary conduct;
- scientific misrepresentation;
- unsupported claims of efficacy, safety, or clinical relevance;
- unsafe operational biological instructions;
- attempts to bypass safety review;
- publishing restricted or unreviewed artifacts;
- hiding failures or negative results for optics;
- deliberately weakening benchmarks to improve appearance;
- misusing agent-generated work to avoid accountability.

## Scientific integrity norm

A contributor who finds a flaw, downgrades a claim, or documents a failure has helped the project.

OpenAMP should reward people who make the repo harder to fool.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, reject, restrict, remove, or revert contributions that violate this code, the safety policy, responsible-use policy, release policy, or claim discipline.

For sensitive issues, use [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md) rather than public discussion.

## Final standard

Be precise, honest, safety-aware, and useful to the next serious reviewer.
