# Evidence Downgrade Guide

When and how to lower the evidence level of a claim.

## When to Downgrade
- New information contradicts the previous evidence.
- The evidence was found to have methodological flaws.
- The evidence cannot be reproduced.
- Caveats were discovered that were previously unknown.

## Levels
See PROOF_LADDER.md for level definitions. Downgrade one level at a time.

## Process
1. Document the reason for the downgrade in the decision log.
2. Update the affected claims and documents.
3. Notify anyone who relied on the previous evidence level.
4. If the downgrade affects a release, block the release until resolved.
