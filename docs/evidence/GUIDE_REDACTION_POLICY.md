# Redaction Policy Guide

When and how to redact content before public sharing.

## What May Need Redaction
- Full peptide sequences for dual-use candidates
- Specific assay protocols from partner CROs
- Personally identifiable information
- Unpublished candidate lists before expert review

## Rules
- Redacted content should be replaced with `[REDACTED]`.
- Note that redaction occurred and why.
- Redaction must not hide negative results or safety issues.
- Original unredacted versions must be preserved in a restricted location.

## What NOT to Redact
- Benchmark results (even negative ones)
- Known limitations
- Safety warnings
- Methodology descriptions
