# Guided Walkthrough: Claim-to-Evidence Matrices

How to map a claim to its supporting evidence.

## Steps
1. State the claim clearly — what exactly is being asserted?
2. Identify the proof level required (see PROOF_LADDER.md).
3. Find the evidence that supports the claim.
4. Check if the evidence level matches the claim strength.
5. If the claim is stronger than the evidence, weaken the claim or gather more evidence.

## Example
| Claim | Required Level | Available Evidence | Match? |
|-------|:--------------:|-------------------|:------:|
| "Candidate X is predicted antimicrobial" | 2 (baseline score) | Ensemble score 0.85 | ✅ |
| "Candidate X is a drug candidate" | 5 (lab assay) | No wet-lab data | ❌ |
